Lakshmana Temple

A Marvel of Chandela Architecture

Lakshmana Temple

Built around 930-950 CE by King Yashovarman of the Chandela dynasty, the Lakshmana Temple is one of the oldest and finest temples at Khajuraho. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, it stands as a testament to the architectural and artistic brilliance of early medieval India.

Architectural Excellence

The temple follows the traditional Nagara style with a tall shikhara (spire) over the sanctum. It is built on a high platform and features a series of beautifully sculpted balconies, intricate ceilings, and walls adorned with thousands of sculptures portraying gods, goddesses, mythical creatures, and daily life.

Sacred Sanctum

The sanctum (garbhagriha) of the Lakshmana Temple houses an exquisite three-headed idol of Vaikuntha Vishnu, a unique form of Vishnu, combining human and animal attributes, emphasizing the cosmic role of the deity.

Artistic Mastery

The outer walls of the temple are richly decorated with over 600 figures arranged in three vertical layers. These include not only divine beings but also musicians, dancers, and intimate human expressions, reflecting the harmonious balance of spiritual and worldly pursuits in Indian philosophy.

Visitor Highlights

Located within the Western Group of Temples at Khajuraho, the Lakshmana Temple remains a key highlight for visitors exploring India's grand medieval architectural heritage.
