Discover Bhedaghat

Bhedaghat, located just 25 km from Jabalpur in Madhya Pradesh, is home to the spectacular marble rocks that rise to a height of 100 feet on either side of the Narmada River. This natural wonder creates a breathtaking gorge that gleams in sunlight and glows under moonlight. The area is also famous for the Dhuandhar Falls, where the Narmada River narrows and plunges from a height, creating a smoky cascade of water. Take a boat ride through the marble canyon, visit the ancient Chausath Yogini temple, or simply marvel at the power and beauty of nature in this magical destination.

Top Attractions

Marble Rock Formations
Marvel at the magnificent white marble cliffs that tower up to 100 feet on both sides of the Narmada River. These formations change color throughout the day, from soft white to golden to pink.
Best Time: October to March
Chausath Yogini Temple
Visit this 10th-century temple dedicated to Goddess Durga and her 64 yogini attendants. Located on a hilltop, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
Timing: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
Dhuandhar Waterfall
Experience the power of the Narmada as it crashes down from a height, creating a misty spray that gives this waterfall its name, which translates to "smoke cascade."
Best Time: Monsoon season (July to September)
Cable Car Ride
Enjoy a panoramic view of the river and marble formations from above on the newly constructed ropeway that connects the two banks of the Narmada River.
Timing: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM

How to Reach

Bhedaghat is located approximately 25 km from Jabalpur city in Madhya Pradesh. The nearest major airport is Jabalpur Airport (JLR), which is about 30 km away. Jabalpur Junction railway station is well-connected to major cities across India. From Jabalpur, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Bhedaghat. The road journey offers scenic views and takes approximately 40 minutes.
