Stupa No. 2

An Early Example of Buddhist Architecture

Stupa No. 2 at Sanchi

Stupa No. 2, located at Sanchi, is an important early Buddhist monument, predating the Great Stupa (Stupa No. 1). It is particularly renowned for its elaborate carvings and its significance in understanding the development of Buddhist art.

Early Architecture

Stupa No. 2 showcases an earlier phase of stupa architecture, characterized by its simpler form compared to the later, more embellished Stupa No. 1.

Exquisite Carvings

The railings surrounding Stupa No. 2 are adorned with intricately carved medallions depicting scenes from the Jataka tales, showcasing early Indian artistry.

Historical Significance

The carvings on Stupa No. 2 provide valuable insights into the social, religious, and cultural life of ancient India during the Shunga period.

A Pilgrim's Journey

While Stupa No. 1 often takes center stage, Stupa No. 2 is a vital part of the Sanchi complex, offering a quieter yet profound experience for visitors interested in Buddhist history and art.
