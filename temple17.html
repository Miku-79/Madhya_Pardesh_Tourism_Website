A Gupta Period Gem at Sanchi

Temple 17 at Sanchi

Temple 17, located at Sanchi, is a significant example of Gupta-period temple architecture. It's a simple yet elegant structure that reflects the classical style of its time and provides valuable insights into the development of temple design in India.

Gupta Architecture

Temple 17 is representative of the Gupta style, characterized by its flat roof, pillared porch, and overall simplicity and balance.

Pillared Structure

The temple features a notable pillared porch or mandapa, which is a common element in Gupta temples and serves as an entrance hall.

Simple Elegance

Unlike some more ornate temples, Temple 17 is admired for its understated elegance and harmonious proportions, highlighting the refined aesthetic of the Gupta period.

Historical Importance

As one of the early structural temples at Sanchi, Temple 17 is crucial for understanding the evolution of temple architecture in India.
