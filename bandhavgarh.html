Bandhavgarh National Park

Discover the ancient forest with the highest density of tigers in India

Welcome to Bandhavgarh National Park

Located in the Umaria district of Madhya Pradesh, Bandhavgarh National Park is one of India's most spectacular wildlife sanctuaries. Covering an area of 105 square kilometers, this biodiverse reserve is known for its remarkable density of Royal Bengal tigers and ancient fort that rises 800 meters above the forest floor. Originally the hunting grounds of the Maharajas of Rewa, Bandhavgarh became a national park in 1968 and has since become a cornerstone of tiger conservation in India. With its stunning landscapes of steep ridges, open meadows, and dense sal forests, Bandhavgarh offers visitors an unforgettable wilderness experience.

Tiger Territory

Bandhavgarh boasts one of the highest densities of Bengal tigers in the world, making it one of the best places to spot these magnificent big cats in their natural habitat. The park's relatively small core area increases your chances of thrilling tiger sightings.

Ancient Heritage

The park is home to the ancient Bandhavgarh Fort, believed to be over 2000 years old. Scattered throughout the park are numerous caves with inscriptions and rock paintings that date back to 1st century BC, offering glimpses into the region's rich history.

Plan Your Visit

Bandhavgarh National Park is open from October 16 to June 30, with the peak wildlife viewing season between March and May.

  • Safari Timings: Morning (6:00 AM - 10:30 AM) and Evening (2:30 PM - 6:00 PM)
  • Getting There: The nearest airport is Jabalpur (190 km), and the nearest railway station is Umaria (35 km)
  • Accommodation: Various options from luxury wildlife lodges to budget-friendly hotels
  • Safari Zones: Tala, Magadhi, and Khitauli zones offer different wildlife experiences
  • Photography: Special photography permits available with advance booking
