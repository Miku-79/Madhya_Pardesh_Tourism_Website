Pench National Park

Experience the real-life setting of Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book"

Welcome to Pench National Park

Nestled in the southern reaches of the Satpura ranges on the border of Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra, Pench National Park derives its name from the pristine Pench River that flows through it. Spreading across 758 square kilometers, this magnificent tiger reserve is believed to be the setting that inspired Rudyard Kipling's most famous work, "The Jungle Book." With its diverse mix of teak forests, open grasslands, and meandering streams, Pench offers a picturesque landscape teeming with wildlife. Home to tigers, leopards, wild dogs, and over 285 species of resident and migratory birds, Pench National Park promises an unforgettable wilderness experience.

Literary Legacy

Believed to be the inspiration for Kipling's "The Jungle Book," Pench's landscape closely resembles the fictional forest of Seoni where Mowgli's adventures took place. Visitors can explore the very jungles that gave birth to one of literature's most beloved tales.

Birdwatcher's Paradise

With over 285 species of birds, including migrants, Pench is a paradise for bird enthusiasts. From the colorful Indian Pitta and Paradise Flycatcher to majestic raptors like the Crested Serpent Eagle and Grey-headed Fish Eagle, the park offers spectacular birding opportunities.

Plan Your Visit

Pench National Park is open from October 1 to June 30, with the best wildlife viewing from February to May.

  • Safari Timings: Morning (6:00 AM - 10:30 AM) and Evening (3:00 PM - 6:00 PM)
  • Getting There: The nearest airport is Nagpur (92 km), and the nearest railway station is Seoni (30 km)
  • Accommodation: Various options from luxury lodges to budget-friendly forest rest houses
  • Safari Zones: Turia, Karmajhiri, and Jamtara offer different wildlife experiences
  • Nature Walks: Guided nature walks available in the buffer zone with advance permission
