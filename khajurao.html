Welcome to Khajuraho

Explore the ancient beauty and history

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Built between 950-1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty, the Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a collection of Hindu and Jain temples renowned for their nagara-style architectural symbolism and exquisite sculptures. These magnificent temples represent one of the finest examples of medieval Indian art and architecture, celebrating life in all its forms - spiritual, sensual, and philosophical.

Architectural Brilliance

The temples of Khajuraho are known for their intricate carvings that cover the outer and inner walls. These masterpieces of Indian art depict various aspects of life, including mythology, daily routines, meditation, spiritual teachings, kinship, wrestlers, and sensual sculptures. Of the original 85 temples, only 25 remain today, spreading over an area of 20 square kilometers in three distinct groups - Western, Eastern, and Southern.

Cultural Significance

The Khajuraho temples embody the essence of Indian philosophy that views life as a harmonious integration of spiritual and physical realms. The temples stand as a testament to the rich cultural, religious, and artistic traditions of medieval India, where different beliefs and practices coexisted. The sculptures not only showcase the masterful craftsmanship of the artisans but also narrate stories from Hindu mythology, revealing the cultural and social fabric of that era.

Western Group

The Western Group of temples is the largest and most important cluster at Khajuraho. This well-maintained complex contains the magnificent Kandariya Mahadeva Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, which rises 31 meters high. Other notable temples in this group include the Lakshmana Temple, Vishvanatha Temple, and Chitragupta Temple, each displaying unique architectural features and sculptural themes that attract art enthusiasts and historians from around the world.

Eastern & Southern Groups

The Eastern Group includes both Hindu and Jain temples, with the Parsvanatha Temple being the largest Jain temple at Khajuraho. The Ghantai Temple, known for its bell (ghanta) sculptures, and the Adinatha Temple are other significant Jain shrines in this group. The Southern Group is smaller but features the beautiful Duladeo Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and the Chaturbhuja Temple with its imposing image of Vishnu.
