Bhopal - City of Lakes

Experience the perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture in the heart of India

Discover Bhopal

Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, is known as the "City of Lakes" for its various natural and artificial lakes. This vibrant city perfectly blends old-world charm with modern urban planning. The old city, with its bustling markets, magnificent mosques, and traditional cuisine, sits to the north of the two lakes, while the new city with its museums, galleries, parks, and gardens stretches to the south. With a rich tapestry of history, architecture, and culture, Bhopal offers travelers a unique glimpse into the heart of India.

Plan Your Visit

Top Attractions

Upper & Lower Lakes
The two connected lakes form the lifeline of Bhopal. Take a boat ride, enjoy the sunset, or simply stroll along the VIP Road that separates them for a refreshing experience.
Boat Rides: 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM
Taj-ul-Masajid
One of Asia's largest mosques, this pink-façaded structure features a grand prayer hall, impressive marble flooring, and two 18-story minarets that dominate Bhopal's skyline.
Open: 6:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Closed during prayer times)
Tribal Museum
A remarkable museum showcasing the life, art, and culture of Madhya Pradesh's tribal communities through innovative displays and immersive exhibits.
Open: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)
Bharat Bhavan
A multi-arts complex and museum designed by architect Charles Correa that houses galleries, workshops, and performance spaces dedicated to preserving and promoting Indian arts.
Open: 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

How to Reach

Bhopal is well-connected to major cities across India through various modes of transportation. Raja Bhoj Airport (BHO) offers daily flights to Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and other major cities. Bhopal Junction Railway Station is a major station on the West Central Railway with trains connecting to all parts of India. The city also has excellent road connectivity through national highways and is served by regular bus services from neighboring states. Within the city, visitors can use the local bus service, auto-rickshaws, taxis, or app-based ride services.
