Khajuraho Dance Festival 2026

The Khajuraho Dance Festival is a week-long celebration of classical Indian dances set against the spectacular backdrop of the illuminated Khajuraho temples. This annual cultural extravaganza brings together the most accomplished classical dancers from across India to perform Kathak, Bharatanatyam, Odissi, Kuchipudi, Manipuri and other classical dance forms. Experience the magic of ancient art forms coming alive amidst the 1000-year-old temples, creating an atmosphere that transcends time and celebrates India's rich cultural heritage.

Festival Venue

The festival takes place at the open-air auditorium in front of the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho. The magnificent temples, built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, provide a spectacular backdrop for the performances. The venue is equipped with excellent acoustics and lighting to enhance the viewing experience while preserving the historical ambiance of this UNESCO World Heritage site.
