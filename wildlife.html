DIVERSE WILDLIFE OF MADHYA PRADESH

Discover The Wilderness of The Heart of India

Madhya Pradesh, known as the "Tiger State of India," boasts exceptional biodiversity across its numerous wildlife sanctuaries and national parks. From the majestic Royal Bengal Tigers to rare bird species, the state offers unparalleled wildlife experiences for nature enthusiasts and wildlife photographers.

With rich deciduous forests, expansive grasslands, and pristine water bodies, Madhya Pradesh creates perfect habitats for a wide variety of flora and fauna. Embark on a journey through the wild heart of India and witness nature's magnificent creations in their natural habitat.

Iconic National Parks

Kanha National Park

Often called the inspiration behind Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book," Kanha National Park encompasses over 940 square kilometers of pristine wilderness. Its meadows and sal forests host an impressive population of tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and the rare hard-ground barasingha (swamp deer).

The park's diverse habitats support over 300 bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. The open grasslands offer excellent wildlife sighting opportunities, especially of the endangered barasingha, which was rescued from near extinction through conservation efforts at Kanha.

Explore Kanha

Bandhavgarh National Park

Boasting one of the highest densities of tigers in the world, Bandhavgarh offers exceptional chances of spotting the royal Bengal tiger in the wild. The ancient Bandhavgarh Fort, standing atop a 2,000-foot plateau, adds historical significance to this natural treasure.

Beyond tigers, the park shelters leopards, various deer species, wild boars, and over 250 species of birds. The terrain's varied topography, including cliffs, grasslands, and dense forest cover, creates diverse microhabitats supporting rich biodiversity.

Explore Bandhavgarh

Pench National Park

Straddling the borders of Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra, Pench National Park features the scenic Pench River flowing through teak forests and open areas. This picturesque landscape provided another inspiration for "The Jungle Book" and is home to a thriving population of tigers, leopards, and wild dogs.

The park's water bodies attract numerous herbivores and over 285 resident and migratory bird species. The riverside habitat offers unique wildlife viewing opportunities not found in other parks of the region.

Explore Pench

Satpura National Park

Set in the rugged Satpura mountain ranges, this national park offers a unique wilderness experience with its distinctive landscape of deep ravines, sandstone peaks, and dense forests. Unlike other parks, Satpura allows walking safaris, giving visitors a more immersive wildlife experience.

The park is known for its population of Indian giant squirrels, sloth bears, leopards, and the occasional tigers. Its less-visited status means wildlife enthusiasts can enjoy nature in relative solitude, away from the crowds found at more popular destinations.

Explore Satpura

Magnificent Wildlife Species

Royal Bengal Tiger

The apex predator of Indian forests, the majestic Bengal tiger roams the wilderness of Madhya Pradesh in significant numbers. Nearly 800 tigers call the state's forests home, making it India's tiger state.

Endangered Carnivore

Indian Leopard

Stealthy and elusive, the Indian leopard shares its habitat with tigers but often remains in the shadows. These powerful predators are known for their adaptability, surviving in dense forests, rocky terrains, and even near human settlements.

Vulnerable Carnivore

Barasingha (Swamp Deer)

Known as the "Jewel of Kanha," the hard-ground barasingha is an endangered deer species found only in Madhya Pradesh. Conservation efforts have led to a remarkable recovery of its population.

Endangered Herbivore

Sloth Bear

These shaggy-coated bears are known for their nocturnal habits and distinctive white chest markings. They primarily feed on insects, fruits, and honey, often using their long claws to dig out termite mounds.
