Tansen Music Festival

A celebration of classical Hindustani music in the birthplace of the legendary musician Tansen

Tansen Music Festival 2025

The Tansen Music Festival is an annual celebration honoring the legendary musical maestro Tansen, one of the Nine Jewels of Emperor Akbar's court. Held in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, this prestigious event brings together the finest classical musicians from across India to perform in the serene surroundings of Tansen's tomb. Experience the magic of Hindustani classical music under the open sky as renowned artists pay homage to the great musician through soul-stirring performances of dhrupad, dhamar, khayal, and instrumental renditions that echo through the historic city of Gwalior.

About Tansen

Tansen, born as Ramtanu Pande, was one of the most influential musicians in Indian history and one of the Nine Jewels (Navratnas) of Emperor Akbar's court. His mastery over Hindustani classical music was legendary - stories tell of his ability to bring rain with Raga Megh Malhar and light lamps with Raga Deepak.


Born in Gwalior, Tansen trained under Swami Haridas and later became the court musician of Raja Ramchandra of Bandavagarh before joining Emperor Akbar's court. His innovations in ragas and his contributions to dhrupad style of singing have shaped Indian classical music for centuries.


The annual Tansen Music Festival held at his tomb in Gwalior pays homage to his unparalleled legacy and keeps the tradition of classical Hindustani music alive for future generations.

Festival Venue

The festival takes place at the historic Tansen's Tomb (Makbara) in Gwalior. This architectural marvel built in the Mughal style houses the final resting place of the legendary musician. The venue provides a serene and spiritually charged atmosphere that enhances the musical experience. The open-air setting allows visitors to enjoy the performances under the stars, just as classical music was traditionally meant to be experienced. The venue is equipped with excellent acoustics while preserving the historical and cultural ambiance of this significant site.
