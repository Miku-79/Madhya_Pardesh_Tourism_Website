Parsvanatha Temple

The Largest Jain Temple at Khajuraho

Parsvanatha Temple

Constructed around 950-970 CE during the reign of the Chandela dynasty, the Parsvanatha Temple is the largest Jain temple in Khajuraho. Originally dedicated to Adinatha, it was later named after Parsvanatha, the 23rd Jain Tirthankara. The temple showcases a beautiful blend of Jain spirituality and intricate sculptural artistry.

Architectural Excellence

Built in the traditional Nagara style, the temple stands on a modest platform and features a sanctum, vestibule, and mandapa. Its exterior walls are elaborately adorned with exquisite carvings, showcasing both religious and secular themes, including divine figures, apsaras (celestial maidens), and everyday scenes.

Sacred Sanctum

The sanctum originally housed an image of Adinatha, but today it contains a statue of Parsvanatha. The serene figure embodies the peaceful and meditative aspects of Jain philosophy.

Artistic Splendor

The temple is celebrated for its delicate and detailed sculptures, some of which are considered among the finest examples of Khajuraho's artistry. Notably, its outer walls include intricate depictions of court life, celestial beings, musicians, dancers, and sensuous figures.

Visitor Highlights

Located within the Eastern Group of Temples at Khajuraho, Parsvanatha Temple remains a major attraction for both devotees and tourists fascinated by Jain heritage and medieval Indian art.
