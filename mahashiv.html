A divine celebration of Lord Shiva in the breathtaking Queen of Satpura

Mahashivratri Fair 2026

The Mahashivratri Fair in Pachmarhi is one of Madhya Pradesh's most vibrant religious and cultural gatherings, drawing thousands of devotees and visitors to the picturesque hill station. This annual celebration honors Lord Shiva with fervent devotion and joyous festivities. As pilgrims ascend to the sacred Chauragarh Temple atop the plateau, the entire region comes alive with spiritual energy, traditional rituals, folk performances, and a colorful fair that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region. Experience the divine atmosphere and breathtaking natural beauty of Pachmarhi during this auspicious occasion.

About Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi, often referred to as 'Satpura ki Rani' (Queen of Satpura), is Madhya Pradesh's only hill station nestled amidst the Satpura Range at an elevation of 1,067 meters. This charming destination is blessed with cascading waterfalls, ancient caves, sandstone peaks, and lush forests that form part of the UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.


The name 'Pachmarhi' is derived from the five ('panch') caves ('marhi') of the Pandava brothers from the Mahabharata epic who are believed to have stayed here during their exile. The British discovered this scenic plateau in 1857 and developed it as a summer retreat.


Beyond its natural splendor and colonial heritage, Pachmarhi holds significant religious importance due to the ancient Chauragarh Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, which becomes the focal point during the Mahashivratri celebrations.

Fair Location

The Mahashivratri Fair is spread across multiple locations in Pachmarhi. The main fair grounds are located in the central area of Pachmarhi town, while the religious ceremonies are centered around the Chauragarh Temple situated atop Chauragarh Hill, approximately 10 km from the town center. The pilgrimage involves a climb of 1,300 steps to reach the ancient temple that houses a massive Shiva Lingam and numerous tridents offered by devotees over the years. The fair transforms the usually serene hill station into a vibrant celebration of faith, culture, and tradition.
