Welcome to Kanha National Park

Nestled in the Maikal range of Satpura hills in Madhya Pradesh, Kanha National Park is one of India's most impressive wildlife reserves. Spanning over 940 square kilometers, this lush sanctuary offers breathtaking landscapes of sal and bamboo forests, rolling meadows, and dense jungle. Kanha is renowned for its successful conservation of the hard-ground barasingha (swamp deer) and is home to significant populations of Bengal tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and over 300 species of birds. The park's natural beauty and biodiversity make it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Rich Biodiversity

Kanha's varied habitats support an incredible diversity of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species. The park is home to over 40 species of mammals, 260 species of birds, and numerous reptiles, amphibians, and insects.

Tiger Reserve

As one of India's premier tiger reserves, Kanha offers some of the best opportunities to spot the majestic Bengal tiger in its natural habitat. The park's effective conservation strategies have helped maintain a healthy tiger population.

Plan Your Visit

Kanha National Park is open from October 1 to June 30, with the best wildlife viewing from February to May.

  • Safari Timings: Morning (6:00 AM - 11:00 AM) and Evening (3:00 PM - 6:00 PM)
  • Getting There: The nearest airport is Jabalpur (160 km), and the nearest railway station is Gondia (145 km)
  • Accommodation: Options range from luxury wildlife resorts to forest rest houses
  • Best Time to Visit: February to May for optimal wildlife sightings
