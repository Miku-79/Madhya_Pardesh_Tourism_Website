Chitragupta Temple

Built around the 11th century CE, the Chitragupta Temple at Khajuraho is unique as it is dedicated to Surya, the Sun God. This temple is celebrated for its rare theme, intricate carvings, and beautifully detailed sculptures that reflect both religious devotion and artistic excellence.

Architectural Features

Constructed in the classic Nagara style, the temple comprises a sanctum, vestibule, maha-mandapa (main hall), and an entrance porch. Its exterior walls are adorned with finely carved panels, showcasing celestial beings, court scenes, dancers, and other symbolic figures.

Sacred Sanctum

Inside the sanctum lies an impressive idol of Surya riding a chariot drawn by seven horses. This grand depiction reflects the sun's cosmic importance and the Hindu belief in Surya's vital role in sustaining life.

Artistic Excellence

Apart from the majestic figure of Surya, the temple's walls are enriched with scenes of royal processions, court musicians, and everyday life, showcasing the diversity and richness of medieval Indian society.

Visitor Highlights

Part of the Western Group of Temples at Khajuraho, Chitragupta Temple is a must-visit for those interested in solar worship and the artistic diversity of India's sacred architecture.
