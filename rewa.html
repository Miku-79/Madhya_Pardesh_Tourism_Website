Rewa - Land of White Tigers

Discover the enchanting heritage, majestic waterfalls, and the legendary home of the rare white tiger

Discover Rewa

Rewa, located in the northeastern part of Madhya Pradesh, is steeped in history and natural beauty. Known as the "Land of White Tigers," it gained international fame when the first white tiger, Mohan, was captured here in 1951. The city is named after the Rewa River (also known as Narmada) that flows nearby. With its rich royal heritage, spectacular waterfalls, ancient temples, and unique wildlife, Rewa offers an authentic cultural experience away from the typical tourist trails. The city harmoniously blends its historical significance with natural wonders, making it a perfect destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and spiritual seekers alike.

Top Attractions

White Tiger Safari
A one-of-a-kind safari and biological park dedicated to the conservation of the rare white tiger. Witness these magnificent creatures in a natural habitat and learn about their history and conservation.
Open: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)
Govindgarh Palace
This historic palace was once the summer residence of the Maharajas of Rewa. It gained fame as the place where Mohan, the first white tiger, was housed by Maharaja Martand Singh.
Open: 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays)
Bahuti Falls
One of the highest waterfalls in India, Bahuti Falls cascades from a height of over 500 feet, creating a spectacular sight especially during the monsoon season.
Best visited: July to October
Venkat Bhavan
A beautifully preserved palace that showcases the architectural brilliance of the Baghel dynasty, featuring intricate carvings, spacious courtyards, and royal chambers.
Open: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Daily)

How to Reach

Rewa is well-connected to major cities in Madhya Pradesh and neighboring states. The nearest airport is Khajuraho Airport (175 km) with limited flights, while Jabalpur Airport (220 km) and Prayagraj Airport (225 km) offer more frequent connections. Rewa Railway Station is well-connected with trains from Delhi, Mumbai, Bhopal, and other major cities. The city is accessible by road via NH30 and NH39, with regular bus services from Bhopal, Jabalpur, Prayagraj, and Varanasi. Within the city, auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available for local transportation.
